Welcome

Welcome to Rising Scholars Head Start Early Learning Center! We are committed to providing high-quality early childhood education that supports the development of the whole child. Through strong family partnerships, engaging learning experiences, and comprehensive support services, we prepare children for success in school and life.

★ Our Mission

At Rising Scholars Head Start, we believe every child deserves a strong start in life. Through nurturing relationships, family partnerships, and developmentally appropriate learning experiences, we support the growth of the whole child — socially, emotionally, physically, cognitively, and academically.

★ Our Vision

Rising Scholars Head Start strives to create a safe, inclusive, and engaging environment where every child is valued, every family is welcomed, and every learner is empowered to reach their fullest potential.

About the Head Start Model

Head Start was established in 1965 to provide comprehensive educational services to young children and their families. The program focuses on school readiness while supporting children's health, nutrition, social-emotional development, and family engagement.
(U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, 2024)

Theoretical Foundation

Bronfenbrenner

Ecological Systems Theory emphasizes that children develop through relationships and environments that include family, school, and community. This is the foundation of Head Start because it promotes strong partnerships between families and educators.

(Bronfenbrenner, 1979)

Vygotsky

Children learn through social interactions with adults and peers. Teachers support learning by guiding children through meaningful conversations, exploration, and collaborative experiences.

(Vygotsky, 1978)

Piaget

Cognitive Development Theory supports hands-on learning experiences. Children construct knowledge through active exploration and play.

(Follari, 2019)

Supporting the Whole Child

What We Nurture

  • Cognitive – Thinking, reasoning, problem-solving
  • Language & Literacy – Communication and early reading
  • Physical – Fine and gross motor skills
  • Social-Emotional – Self-regulation and relationships
  • Health & Nutrition – Healthy habits and wellness
  • Family Engagement – Strong home-school partnerships
  • School Readiness – Preparing for future academic success

Why We Chose Head Start

The Head Start model was selected because it recognizes that children learn best when families, educators, and communities work together. The program provides comprehensive services that support children's academic, physical, social, and emotional development while empowering families to become active participants in their children's education.

(U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, 2024)

Many families in our community benefit from access to educational resources, health services, family engagement opportunities, and individualized support. Head Start's comprehensive approach ensures that children receive the services they need to thrive.

Our Classrooms

👶 Infant Classroom (Birth–12 Months)

Curriculum, Instruction & Activities

Infants learn through sensory experiences, responsive caregiving, language interactions, music, and movement.

Supporting Theories

  • Piaget – Sensorimotor learning
  • Maslow – Meeting basic needs and developing trust
  • Bronfenbrenner – Family partnerships

Materials & Resources

  • Soft mats, mirrors, sensory toys, board books, cribs, rocking chairs

Parent Volunteer Opportunities

  • Reading books, participating in family events, sharing family traditions

At-Home Activities

  • Singing songs and nursery rhymes
  • Reading daily to your baby
  • Tummy time for physical development
  • Talking during daily routines (bath time, feeding, diapering)

What Families Will See and Hear

Nurturing teacher-child interactions, gentle music, language-rich conversations, and individualized care that meets each baby's unique needs.

👦 Toddler Classroom (1–2 Years)

Curriculum, Instruction & Activities

Toddlers engage in dramatic play, sensory exploration, language development activities, and hands-on learning experiences that support their growing independence.

Supporting Theories

  • Vygotsky – Learning through social interaction
  • Piaget – Exploration and discovery through play

Materials & Resources

  • Blocks, dramatic play center, art materials, sensory bins, picture books

Parent Volunteer Opportunities

  • Story time, family engagement nights, classroom helper, sharing cultural traditions

At-Home Activities

  • Sorting objects by color, shape, or size
  • Singing nursery rhymes with actions
  • Outdoor exploration and nature walks
  • Vocabulary-building conversations during daily routines

What Families Will See and Hear

Active exploration, teacher-guided learning, songs and movement activities, and cooperative play with peers.

📚 Preschool Classroom (3–4 Years)

Curriculum, Instruction & Activities

Children participate in literacy, math, science, social studies, art, music, and play-based learning experiences that prepare them for kindergarten.

Supporting Theories

  • Vygotsky – Teacher scaffolding and guided learning
  • Piaget – Hands-on discovery learning
  • Bronfenbrenner – Family-school partnerships

Materials & Resources

  • Learning centers, STEM materials, writing supplies, books, manipulatives, art supplies

Parent Volunteer Opportunities

  • Reading to children, assisting with projects, family engagement nights, field trip chaperones, sharing special skills or hobbies

At-Home Activities

  • Reading together daily
  • Counting activities with everyday objects
  • Letter recognition games
  • Nature walks with observation
  • Simple cooking and measuring activities

What Families Will See and Hear

Play-based instruction, small-group learning, collaborative projects, teacher-child discussions, and regular family conferences.

Frequently Asked Questions

📱 How will I receive information if I don't have internet access?

We share information through printed newsletters, flyers, phone calls, text messages, Class Dojo, parent-teacher conferences, and family engagement events.

🌎 How do you support families who speak languages other than English?

We provide translated materials when possible and use translation tools or interpreters when needed. We want every family to feel comfortable communicating with our center.

🚀 How will the center be advertised?

We advertise through social media, community events, local libraries, churches, and other community organizations. We also encourage current families to share information with friends and relatives.

👥 How do you encourage families from different family structures to participate?

We use inclusive language in all communications and create family engagement opportunities that are welcoming to everyone. Our classroom books, materials, and displays reflect different types of families so that every child feels represented.
